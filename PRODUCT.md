# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Developers, operators, and infrastructure engineers who work with remote servers from a desktop environment. They need to move between direct SSH hosts and enterprise bastions, inspect terminal output quickly, transfer files, and turn recurring procedures into repeatable scripts without losing the immediacy of a real terminal.

## Product Purpose

AI Term is a focused SSH workbench that keeps terminal access, bastion routing, SFTP, operational context, and reusable scripts in one local desktop application. The terminal remains the source of truth. AI improves comprehension and reuse by explaining selected output, summarizing logs, drafting commands, and turning recorded operations into editable scripts, while execution stays reviewable and user-controlled.

## Brand Personality

Direct, calm, and technically credible. The product should feel compact enough for daily use, readable under pressure, and confident without making AI the center of attention.

## Anti-references

AI Term should not resemble a chat-first AI wrapper, a decorative developer landing page, or a dense enterprise admin console. It should avoid novelty terminal effects, oversized marketing typography, ornamental dashboards, and automation that obscures the exact command or terminal output being acted on.

## Design Principles

- SSH first, AI in support: keep the live terminal and connection state primary.
- Show operational proof: use real output, commands, transfer state, and script readiness instead of abstract claims.
- Preserve readable density: fit the full workflow on screen without compressing terminal text or hiding critical state.
- Keep actions reviewable: surface command content, risk, target, and progress before consequential operations.
- Reduce context switching: connect, inspect, explain, record, script, and transfer within one workspace.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Maintain readable contrast in both light and dark themes, preserve semantic labels for icon controls, support reduced-motion preferences, and never rely on color alone for connection, risk, or transfer status. Terminal and code text must remain selectable and legible at the application minimum window size.
